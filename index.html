Taras Shevchenko

A person who has been inspiring Ukrainians for more than one century

Here's a time line of Taras Shevchenko life:

  • 1814 - Born in the village of Moryntsi, Kiev Governorate
  • 1822 - Was sent to a school, where he was taught to read and write
  • 1838 - He is redeemed from serfdom and becomes a student of the Academy of Arts
  • 1840 - Published the first collection of his poetic works under the name "Kobzar"
  • 1844 - Wrote the highly political poem "Dream"
  • 1846 - Became a member of the Cyril and Methodius brotherhood
  • 1847 - They were arrested and sent to St. Petersburg and imprisoned in a casemate
  • 1848 - He was included in the Aral expedition
  • 1850 - He was arrested a second time and, after six months in prison, imprisoned in the Novopetrovsky coastal fort
  • 1859 - Returned to Ukraine, which he had not seen for 12 years
  • 1861 - Died in St. Petersburg. He was buried at first in the Smolensk cemetery, but according to the "Testament", on May 10 of the same year, his ashes were reburied in Ukraine above the Dnieper on Chernechaya Gora (Kaniv)

He was the son of a peasant - and became a ruler in the kingdom of the Spirit. He was a serf - and became a giant in the realm of human culture

--Ivan Franko Ukrainian poet, writer
